ETHICS IN AI

1. Who is ultimately responsible for the moral consequences of an AI system's decisions?

Correct Option: B) The creators and developers of the AI system.

Solution and Explanation:
The responsibility for AI's actions generally falls on its creators, developers, and the organizations that deploy the systems. Although AI systems can make autonomous decisions, the people and companies behind them are accountable for ensuring these systems are ethical, transparent, and fair.

**Key Considerations**:
1. Accountability for design choices.
2. Addressing biases in AI training.
3. Continuous monitoring for unintended consequences.




2. Which ethical principle should guide the design and deployment of AI systems?

Correct Option: B) Beneficence, ensuring AI benefits humanity.

Solution and Explanation:
The principle of **beneficence** dictates that AI systems should be designed and deployed to promote human well-being and the common good. This includes preventing harm, promoting fairness, and ensuring the technology serves all members of society equitably.

**Key Ethical Principles in AI**:
1. Beneficence – promoting well-being.
2. Justice – ensuring fairness and non-discrimination.
3. Autonomy – respecting individual rights and freedoms.




3. How can we ensure that AI algorithms are fair and free from bias?

Solution and Explanation:

**Approaches to Bias Reduction**:
1. Curate balanced and representative datasets.
2. Regularly audit AI systems for bias.
3. Implement fairness-aware algorithms.
