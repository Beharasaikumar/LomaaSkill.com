1. What are the two main components of a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN)?

Correct Option: B) Generator and Discriminator

Solution and Explanation:
A Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) consists of two main components: the Generator and the Discriminator. The Generator creates data, while the Discriminator tries to distinguish between real and generated data.

**Steps in GAN Operation**:
1. The Generator creates fake data.
2. The Discriminator evaluates the authenticity of the data.
3. The Generator improves based on feedback from the Discriminator.




2. In GANs, which component is responsible for generating new data?

Correct Option: C) Generator

Solution and Explanation:
In a GAN, the Generator is responsible for creating new data by generating fake samples that resemble real data.

**Steps in Generator Operation**:
1. The Generator receives random noise.
2. It processes this noise to create data that appears similar to real data.
3. The Discriminator evaluates this data.




3. Which of the following is a key challenge in training GANs?

Correct Option: B) Mode collapse

Solution and Explanation:
Mode collapse is a problem where the Generator produces limited outputs, essentially “collapsing” to a small variety of generated data, which makes the GAN less effective at creating diverse data.

**Challenges in GAN Training**:
1. Mode collapse.
2. Difficulty in balancing the Generator and Discriminator.
3. Instability during training.

8. What distinguishes Conditional GANs (cGANs) from traditional GANs?

Correct Option: D) They allow conditioning on specific information (e.g., class labels)

Solution and Explanation:
Conditional GANs (cGANs) extend traditional GANs by conditioning both the generator and discriminator on additional information, such as class labels or other specific details, which allows for more controlled and diverse output generation.

**Conditioning in cGANs**:
1. The generator receives both random noise and conditioning data.
2. The discriminator also takes the conditioning data into account when determining whether data is real or fake.

19. In the context of reinforcement learning, which term describes the reward given to the generator in a GAN?

Correct Option: D) Reward signal

Solution and Explanation:
In reinforcement learning, the generator in a GAN receives a "reward signal" from the discriminator or environment. This reward guides the generator to improve its outputs by giving feedback on how realistic the generated data is.

**Key Terms in RL for GANs**:
1. **Reward Signal**: The feedback that tells the generator how good its output is.
2. **Discriminator Score**: Represents how well the discriminator distinguishes between real and fake data.
3. **Q-value**: A value used in reinforcement learning for state-action pairs (not typically for GANs).




20. What is the key feature of diffusion models in generative AI?

Correct Option: A) They generate data in a stepwise process starting from noise

Solution and Explanation:
Diffusion models generate data by progressively denoising a noisy signal over a series of steps. The process starts with random noise and gradually refines it into structured data (e.g., an image), effectively reversing the diffusion process.

**How Diffusion Models Work**:
1. Start from random noise and progressively refine the data.
2. Denoise the data step by step to reveal the final output.
3. Used in image generation, such as generating realistic images from noise.
