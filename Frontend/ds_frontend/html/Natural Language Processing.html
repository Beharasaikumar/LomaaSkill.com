Natural Language Processing

1. What is the primary goal of text preprocessing in NLP?

Correct Option: B) To clean and prepare the text data for analysis and modeling.

Solution and Explanation:
Text preprocessing involves cleaning and preparing text data by removing unwanted characters, normalizing text, and splitting it into useful components to help models better understand the data.

**Key Steps in Text Preprocessing:**
1. Lowercasing
2. Tokenization
3. Removing stopwords
4. Lemmatization/Stemming




2. Which of the following is a common technique used to remove unnecessary words in text preprocessing?

Correct Option: B) Removing stopwords

Solution and Explanation:
Stopwords are common words (like "and", "the", "is") that don't provide significant meaning in the analysis. Removing them helps reduce the size of the data and makes the model more efficient.

**Common Text Preprocessing Techniques:**
1. Lowercasing
2. Tokenization
3. Removing stopwords
4. Lemmatization/Stemming




3. What does tokenization refer to in text preprocessing?

Correct Option: B) Splitting text into smaller components such as words or sentences.

Solution and Explanation:
Tokenization is the process of breaking down text into individual components like words or sentences. These tokens are then used for further analysis or modeling.

**Types of Tokenization:**
1. Word Tokenization
2. Sentence Tokenization
3. Subword Tokenization




4. What is the purpose of stemming in text preprocessing?

Correct Option: B) To reduce words to their root form.

Solution and Explanation:
Stemming involves reducing words to their base or root form (e.g., "running" becomes "run"). This helps to group similar words together for better analysis.

**Common Stemming Algorithms:**
1. Porter Stemmer
2. Snowball Stemmer
3. Lancaster Stemmer




5. Which of the following is an example of lemmatization in text preprocessing?

Correct Option: A) Reducing "better" to "good".

Solution and Explanation:
Lemmatization is the process of converting words into their base or dictionary form (e.g., "better" becomes "good"). Unlike stemming, lemmatization uses context to determine the correct base form.

**Common Lemmatization Libraries:**
1. WordNet Lemmatizer
2. Spacy Lemmatizer




6. What is the purpose of text representation in NLP?

Correct Option: B) To convert text into a numerical format that can be used by machine learning algorithms.

Solution and Explanation:
Text representation involves converting raw text data into a structured format (such as numerical vectors) that machine learning models can interpret and process.

**Common Text Representation Techniques:**
1. Bag of Words (BoW)
2. Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF)
3. Word Embeddings (e.g., Word2Vec, GloVe)




7. Which of the following is a common method for representing text in NLP?

Correct Option: B) TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency)

Solution and Explanation:
TF-IDF is a popular technique used for text representation that measures the importance of a word in a document relative to a collection of documents.

**Text Representation Techniques:**
1. Bag of Words (BoW)
2. TF-IDF
3. Word2Vec
4. GloVe




8. Which of the following is a drawback of the Bag of Words (BoW) model?

Correct Option: B) It ignores the order of words in the text.

Solution and Explanation:
The Bag of Words (BoW) model is a simple text representation method, but it ignores word order, which can lead to the loss of important context in some cases.

**Drawbacks of BoW:**
1. Ignores word order.
2. Can create a large sparse matrix.
3. Doesn’t capture semantic meaning.




9. What does Word2Vec do in text representation?

Correct Option: B) It maps words to dense vector representations based on their context in a text corpus.

Solution and Explanation:
Word2Vec is a technique that learns distributed representations of words, where each word is represented by a vector. This model captures semantic relationships between words.

**Word2Vec Models:**
1. Continuous Bag of Words (CBOW)
2. Skip-gram




10. What is the advantage of using GloVe for text representation?

Correct Option: B) It captures global statistical information of the corpus and constructs word vectors based on co-occurrence.

Solution and Explanation:
GloVe (Global Vectors for Word Representation) captures global statistical information of a corpus by factoring the word co-occurrence matrix. It is highly effective for capturing relationships between words.

**GloVe Features:**
1. Captures global co-occurrence patterns.
2. Produces dense word vectors.
3. Effective for semantic similarity tasks.

13. What is the key feature of the Naive Bayes algorithm for text classification?

Correct Option: B) It assumes that the features (words) are conditionally independent given the class.

61. What is the main task of coreference resolution in NLP?

Correct Option: B) To identify which words in a text refer to the same entity.

Solution and Explanation:
Coreference resolution is the process of identifying words or phrases in a text that refer to the same entity, such as resolving pronouns like "he," "she," "it," to their antecedents (e.g., "John," "the cat").

**Example:**
"John went to the store. He bought some milk." Here, "He" refers to "John."




62. Which of the following is a common challenge in coreference resolution?

Correct Option: B) Ambiguity in pronoun references.

Solution and Explanation:
Ambiguity arises when a pronoun or noun phrase could refer to multiple entities in the text. For example, in the sentence "Sarah told Jane that she would be late," it's unclear whether "she" refers to Sarah or Jane.

**Other challenges include:**
1. Handling long-distance coreference.
2. Identifying non-pronoun references (e.g., "the president").




63. Which of the following is an example of a coreference resolution task?

Correct Option: B) Resolving the pronoun "it" to "the book".

Solution and Explanation:
Coreference resolution involves identifying which noun or phrase a pronoun refers to. For example, in the sentence "I finished the book. It was great," "it" refers to "the book".

**Other examples include:**
1. "John saw Mary. He waved to her." (John → He, Mary → her)




64. Which of the following techniques is commonly used in coreference resolution?

Correct Option: B) Machine learning-based approaches.

Solution and Explanation:
Coreference resolution is often tackled using machine learning techniques such as supervised learning, where models are trained on labeled datasets to predict coreference links. Other techniques include rule-based methods and deep learning approaches.

**Common algorithms:**
1. Support Vector Machines (SVMs)
2. Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)
3. Conditional Random Fields (CRFs)




65. What is a common evaluation metric for coreference resolution systems?

The F1 score is commonly used to evaluate the performance of coreference resolution systems. It is the harmonic mean of precision and recall and provides a balance between them.

**Other evaluation metrics:**
1. Precision
2. Recall
3. Accuracy
